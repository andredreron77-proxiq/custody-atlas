Add a simple first-time onboarding walkthrough to Custody Atlas for new users.

Goal:
Help first-time users immediately understand what Custody Atlas does, what they can do next, and how to get value quickly.

Requirements:

========================================
PART 1 — First-Time Welcome Experience
========================================

1. Detect first-time users.
Use a simple MVP approach such as:
- localStorage flag
- or a lightweight user profile field if already available

2. On first visit after login, show a welcome onboarding modal or guided card overlay.

Title:
“Welcome to Custody Atlas”

Subtitle:
“Understand custody law where you live, organize your case, and ask better questions.”

========================================
PART 2 — Explain the 3 Core Actions
========================================

3. Show three quick onboarding steps:

Step 1:
“Ask Atlas”
Description:
“Ask custody questions based on your location and continue the conversation.”

Step 2:
“Analyze a Document”
Description:
“Upload a custody order or legal document and get a plain-English explanation.”

Step 3:
“Use Your Workspace”
Description:
“Your questions, documents, and progress are saved so you can return anytime.”

4. Use simple icons or visual cards for each step.

========================================
PART 3 — Suggested First Actions
========================================

5. Provide clear CTA buttons in the onboarding flow, such as:
- “Ask My First Question”
- “Upload a Document”
- “Go to Workspace”

6. If jurisdiction is not set yet, prioritize:
- “Set My Location”

========================================
PART 4 — Optional Tips
========================================

7. Include a small note:
“Custody Atlas provides general legal information, not legal advice.”

8. Include a small trust message:
“Your documents and conversations are tied to your account and handled privately.”

========================================
PART 5 — Dismiss / Reopen
========================================

9. Allow the user to:
- close onboarding
- not see it again automatically after completion

10. Add a way to reopen it later, such as:
- a “Help” or “Getting Started” link in the navigation or profile menu

========================================
PART 6 — UX / Design
========================================

11. Keep the onboarding lightweight:
- no more than 1–2 screens
- clean, modern, mobile-friendly
- aligned with the Custody Atlas visual style

12. Do not interrupt users repeatedly after first completion.

========================================
PART 7 — Do Not Break
========================================

13. Preserve:
- auth flow
- Ask Atlas
- workspace
- document analysis
- usage limits
- admin features

========================================
PART 8 — Summary
========================================

14. After implementation, summarize:
- files changed
- how first-time users are detected
- how the onboarding is dismissed and remembered
- where users can reopen it later
