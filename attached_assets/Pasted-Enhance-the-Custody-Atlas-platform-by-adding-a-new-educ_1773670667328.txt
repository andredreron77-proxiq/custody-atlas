Enhance the Custody Atlas platform by adding a new educational feature explaining how custody arrangements may influence child support.

Goal:
Provide users with plain-English explanations of how custody and parenting time may impact child support in their jurisdiction without building a full support calculator.

Requirements:

1. Create a reusable component called ChildSupportImpactCard.

2. Display this card on the following pages:

* jurisdiction law summary page
* AI answer results (when relevant)
* document analysis results if the document references child support

3. Card Content Structure:

Title:
"Custody and Child Support"

Description:
Explain that child support laws vary by state and that parenting time can influence support obligations.

Include examples such as:

* If one parent has the child most of the time, the other parent may pay more support.
* Shared custody arrangements may change support amounts depending on income and expenses.

4. Jurisdiction Awareness

If jurisdiction data exists, display the support calculation model used by that state when available.

Example:
"Georgia generally uses the Income Shares Model for calculating child support."

Use placeholder data if the dataset does not yet include this field.

5. Add a CTA button:
   "Ask about child support in your state"

This button should open the AI question interface with the jurisdiction context.

6. UX Design

Use the same card design style as other components:

* white card
* subtle shadow
* rounded corners
* professional legal-tech appearance

7. Trust Messaging

Include a small note at the bottom:

"Custody Atlas provides educational information about custody and child support. It does not replace legal advice from a licensed attorney."

8. Maintain existing functionality

Do not modify the current custody law dataset or AI routes unless necessary.

9. After implementation

Provide a summary of:

* new components created
* pages where the card appears
* any placeholder data used.
